Now for the mix up!

Wait... what is responsive web design?

Responsive web design is a pretty lofty term... Wikipedia defines RWD as Web design approach aimed at crafting sites to provide an optimal viewing experience — easy reading and navigation with a minimum of resizing, panning, and scrolling—across a wide range of devices (from desktop computer monitors to mobile phones).

What made up the web pre-RWD?

This is what the web use to be, on an old desktop computer just like this. it was around a 1024x768... or 1400x1050... or 800x600... what ever the size was we thought 960px was the perfect size for our websites and life was good! btw I started making and consuming websites on this computer. as soon as we get 960 dialed in new devices start to enter the picture

What make up the web now?

phone tablets mini laptops hd laptops mega screens with a gillion pixels o yea... and that same old computer this is the web now... we still don't know exactly what to do about it, do we build native apps? do we build mobile versions of the site? do we go responsive? do we just ignore the problem? ... the joy of going responsive is we build our sites for a couple sizes, and all of a suddon we are prepaired for our fringe use cases.

What make up the web in the future?

watch game consoles smart tv and of course all the things we have now. all these things exists but we don't credit it for being the web. This creates huge problems, because if you are building native apps, or device spesific sites...all of a sudon we need to have a car app, and a game console app, and a smart tv app... - each of these could run their own os, or have their own mobile interface, when we are designing we can't plan for everything to fit in side our own box...

What make up the web in the future?

fridge car browser picture frame - add things that are a bit outside of that (refrigerators, paintball guns, door locks, alarm clocks, and AC units and a ton of question marks) - crazy things are coming in the future, and chances are EVERYTHING will connect to the INTERNET. and if we don't get ahead of them we will always be placed on the reactive position...

so, why its good for designers?!

  • Does that mean things are more confusing?
  • Does that mean more work?
  • So why do we like this?
    • Greater Impact
    • On a whole less work
This makes things more confusing, and over all more daunting... we don't want to have to rewrite for every new app that comes out, we want the things we make now to work on devices coming out next Christmas! Why is this good for designers? doesn't that just mean more work? sadly it does mean more work, but it also means we get to design for the future through responsive and adaptive webdesign, making us and our work more impactful and valuable.

Ups and downs of responsive

show original iphone, a treo 650, and another old phone... Original devices where slow as shit treo 650 312 MHz 32mb ram 320 x 320 pixels iphone - original iphone 413 mhz - 128mb ram - 320x480 resolution Bad connections - network speeds are still not super fast... but man... could you imagine... - 70 to 150 kbps was great on edge original with bad hardware and bad networks... These limitations forced the original creators of mobile websites to start making decisions about what people wanted. we knew that if people had to wait on all the features of the full site it would take for every, so we started cutting things out... and in the original sites we ended up delivering a cold bottle of
web lite --- (same-ish taiste) half the features - we learned from those original experiences that people don't want all their options yanked away - which is really scary because according to compuware "57 percent of consumers will not recommend a business with a poorly designed mobile site. - Similarly, 40 percent of consumers will go to a competitor’s site after a bad mobile experience"
